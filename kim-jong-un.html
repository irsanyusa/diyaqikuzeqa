Kim Jong-un (Supreme Leader and Dictator of North Korea)

Profession: Supreme Leader and Dictator of North Korea

Biography: Kim Jong Un is the leader of North Korea and the third generation of the Kim dynasty to rule the country. He has overseen significant advancements in North Korea's nuclear weapons program and engaged in high-profile diplomacy with world leaders.

Kim was born in the early 1980s as the third son of Kim Jong Il. He spent part of his youth in Switzerland, where he attended school under an alias. Classmates reported that he enjoyed playing basketball during this time.

In 2009, Kim was chosen as his father's successor. He took power in 2011 after his father's death, despite being the youngest son. Kim quickly moved to secure his position, executing his uncle Jang Song Thaek in 2013.

Under Kim's rule, North Korea accelerated its nuclear weapons and missile programs. The country conducted multiple tests and claimed to have developed missiles capable of reaching the United States. Kim also introduced some economic reforms, including giving more autonomy to businesses.

In 2018, Kim engaged in unprecedented diplomacy. He held summits with South Korean President Moon Jae-in and met with President Donald Trump. These meetings marked a significant shift in North Korea's international relations.

Kim has cultivated a more public image than his predecessors, often appearing with his wife Ri Sol Ju. He has shown an interest in Western culture, hosting former basketball player Dennis Rodman on several occasions.
